# Research Orientation Exercises

## Paper Anatomy

1. Pick one paper from `docs/research/references.yaml` and state its main claim in one sentence.
2. Name the bottleneck or limitation the paper says it addresses.
3. Describe the mathematical mechanism or inductive bias the paper adds.
4. State whether the main change is best described as an objective change, an architecture change, or both.

## Evidence And Limits

5. List the strongest piece of evidence the paper provides for its claim.
6. Name one limitation, missing control, or external-validity concern in that evidence.
7. Propose one toy replication that isolates the paper's mechanism on a smaller task.

## Experiment Design

8. Define a baseline for the toy replication.
9. Choose one primary metric and one paired comparison you would compute on the same evaluation examples.
10. Name one ablation that should reduce performance if the proposed mechanism actually matters.

## Critique

11. Write a short critique with four labeled sentences: claim, mechanism, evidence, limitation.
12. State one failure criterion that would make you stop trusting the toy replication.
